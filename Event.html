HIGH TECHNOLOGY

High technology (high tech or high-tech), also known as advanced technology (advanced tech) or exotechnology,[failed verification] is technology that is at the cutting edge: the highest form of technology available.[2] It can be defined as either the most complex or the newest technology on the market.[3] The opposite of high tech is low technology, referring to simple, often traditional or mechanical technology; for example, a slide rule is a low-tech calculating device.[4][5][6] When high tech becomes old, it becomes low tech, for example vacuum tube electronics. Further, high tech is related to the concept of mid-tech, that is a balance between the two opposite extreme qualities of low-tech and high tech. SPORTS CULTURAL

Cultural Influence on Sports

Culture impacts the very nature of sporting activities—from rules to playing styles and how they are celebrated. Think of indigenous sports like the Native American game of lacrosse or China's traditional martial art of wushu; these sports have firm foundations in cultural practices and contexts shaped by history. The sports bear imprints of values, beliefs, and historical experiences from their original communities, which cut to show that culture may shape athletic traditions and athletic practice.

Sports as Cultural Expression

Sports are an influential means of cultural expression, through which a society projects its values, identity, and group unity. It is easy to find major sporting events imbued with cultural rites, icons, and customs that are indicative of celebrations of cultural identity. For example, opening ceremonies of the Olympics or halftime programs of the Super Bowl often display acts that underline cultural symbols of the host nation and stress that sports are an avenue of cultural expression.

Globalization and Cultural Exchange

Globalization has facilitated tremendous cultural exchange through sports in that it brings different cultures involved in international competitions together. Among other activities, the FIFA World Cup and the Olympic Games are events that open avenues for sharing and learning various traditions among different nations. This exchange, due to the fact that people from diverse backgrounds come into contact and learn from each other through the universal language of sports, fosters cross-cultural understanding and appreciation.

Impact on Social Norms and Values

Sports can play an instrumental role in shaping and challenging social norms and values. With the visibility of female athletes increasing across sports and with the promotion of diversity in this sector, it has managed to be one of the greater pushes of broader societal shifts concerning gender roles, inclusivity, and equality. For example, the development of women's professional sports leagues challenges the traditional stereotypes that existed against women in sports and also, more generally, toward traditional attitudes about gender or race through the increased visibility of the players as athletes coming from diverse backgrounds.
